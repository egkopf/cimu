When Kaci Xie was a child, most of her clothes were frilly, victorian-inspired hand-me-downs from her cousin that were two sizes too big. Knowing that the clothes weren't going to help her blend in in suburban Georgia, Kaci taught herself how to alter her clothes by watching YouTube videos. This childhood practice caused Kaci to develop a strong interest in clothing utilization and ultimately led her to build Cimu.

What We Do

Reduce Reverse Logistics

Our alterations and network of tailors ensure that only clothes with issues that can't be solved with a tailor end up in your returns pile. By reforming your returns process, we are reducing the number of returns overall.

Improve Customer Experience

Cimu can turn garments from something customers like into something customers love. By allowing your customers to tailor their clothes to perfection, we're helping them feel more empowered and confident wearing your apparel. Additionally, over time, we will be able to collect microdata from our alterations that will better inform the manufacturing process.

Encourage Sustainability

By making sure clothes successfully enter customers' closets, we're ensuring that clothes don't end up in landfills. We are increasing clothing utilization and decreasing clothing waste. In doing so, we are driving the heart of a circular economy.

Empower Local Communities

Our network of tailors include traditional tailors and community members. Many community members don't have the economic means to create a tailoring shop but have sewing capabilities that rival those with storefronts. By employing former unhoused folks, refugees, and stay-at-home moms, we're empowering the local community through economic means.
